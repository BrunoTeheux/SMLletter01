Math Day

March 1, 2025

On March 1st, 150 high-school students came to Campus Belval of the University of Luxembourg for the Math Day. In the morning, they participated in a mathematical competition while in the afternoon they attended mathematical workshops in smaller groups. The winners of the competition received book prizes and will be admitted to more advanced mathematical training sessions.

GEM - Girls Exploring Math

May 8, 2025

The next edition of GEM (Girls Exploring Math) will take place on Campus Belval on May 8. This annual event gathers female high school students for one day to show them that mathematics is also for them. Participants will meet mathematicians, explore mathematical universes and debunk gender stereotypes in STEM.

Registration will soon be opened on the website of GEM.

Journées Arithmétiques

June 30 to July 4, 2025

The Journées Arithmétiques is the largest mathematical conference worldwide that focuses on number theory. This year, it will be hosted in Luxembourg for the first time. On Thursday, July 3rd, there will be a public lecture by Dr. Kristin Lauter (at Meta: Senior Director of FAIR Labs North America) on the new era of AI for mathematics.

All information can be found on the conference website JA25.

Multiplicities Talk by Hugo Parlier

June 4, 2025

Save the date! Prof. Hugo Parlier, who recently left Luxembourg for Fribourg University, will be back for a general audience talk in the Multiplicities Seminar on June 4, 2025 on Campus Belval.

He's going to surprise us with a talk at the intersection of art and mathematics. Stay tuned on the Multiplicities website for further information.

Twelve Fantastic Stories

February 28, 2025

The book by Prof. Antonella Perucca and Dr. Lisa Vagnozzi, Twelve Fantastic Stories, is now freely available online in English, French, German, and Italian. In the twelve short stories, the characters solve mathematical riddles in a cooperative way. You can find twelve classical and accessible riddles for all ages. To access the book please visit the dedicated website.

SML Prizes

December, 2024

Congratulations to Béatrice Bach (Master in Secondary Education - Mathematics) and Camal Tahireddine for winning the SML prize for the best student in the Masters in Mathematics, and in the Bachelor of Mathematics, respectively. Both prizes, offered yearly by the SML, are awarded to students of the University of Luxembourg.
