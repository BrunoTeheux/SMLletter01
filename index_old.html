President's Message

March 1, 2025

To do: careers day, Jean-Baptiste Aubin, event of February

Dear Members, Friends, and Mathematics Enthusiasts,

The Luxembourg Mathematical Society (SML) is proud to launch this newsletter, a new initiative aimed at sharing news and developments in mathematics, both within Luxembourg and beyond.

We hope this will become a space for discussion, inspiration, and exchange among all those who are passionate about mathematical ideas.

Mathematics is everywhere—perhaps more so now than ever before. As we find ourselves in the midst of a technological revolution, with artificial intelligence, machine learning, and data science transforming our world, mathematics stands as one of the indispensable pillars of these advancements.

The SML will do its best to spark curiosity, encourage exchange, and support all those who practice, teach, or simply love mathematics. We are also proud to represent Luxembourg on the international stage, through our affiliation with organizations such as the International Mathematical Union (IMU).

After a few years of relatively low activity, the SML has reinvigorated its mission, organizing events that appeal to the entire mathematical community. For example, on December 2nd, 2024, we hosted a fantastic event featuring Jean-Baptiste Aubin, who captivated the audience with wonderful demonstrations of mathematical magic—a perfect illustration of how mathematics can surprise and inspire. On February 5th, 2025, the SML co-sponsored an evening for the general public on statistics and probability.

But we need your input, ideas, and enthusiasm! Mathematics is a collective endeavour, and its impact reaches far beyond academia—it shapes industries, education, and society at large. Whether you are a researcher, a student, a teacher, or simply someone who finds joy in mathematical thinking, your perspective matters.

We encourage you to read our newsletter and discover how mathematical initiatives can have a lasting impact on society. And, most importantly, we invite you to join the SML—help us grow Luxembourg’s mathematical community, share your insights, and take part in our activities.

Membership instructions can be found at on the SML's website .

Looking forward to engaging with you all,

Prof. Giovanni Peccati
President of the Luxembourg Mathematical Society

Math Day

March 1, 2025

Math enthusiasts are welcome in Belval

On March 1st, 100 high-school students came to Campus Belval at the University of Luxembourg for the Math Day. The morning featured a mathematical competition, while the afternoon offered three mathematical workshops in smaller groups.

The participants had the opportunity to connect with early-career mathematicians and discover mathematical concepts beyond their standard school curriculum."

GEM - Girls Exploring Math

May 8, 2025

Breaking barriers in mathematics

The next edition of GEM (Girls Exploring Math) will take place on Campus Belval on May 8. This vibrant annual event brings together female high school students for a day of discovery, showing them that mathematics is very much their domain too. Participants will connect with practicing mathematicians, dive into fascinating mathematical universes, and challenge gender stereotypes in STEM fields.

Registration will soon be opened on the website of GEM.

Journées Arithmétiques

June 30 to July 4, 2025

Number theory takes center stage in Luxembourg

The Journées Arithmétiques, the world's largest mathematical conference dedicated to number theory, will be hosted in Luxembourg for the first time this year. Mark your calendar for Thursday, July 3rd, when Dr. Kristin Lauter (Senior Director of FAIR Labs North America at Meta) will deliver a fascinating public lecture on the new era of AI for mathematics.

All information can be found on the conference website of the JA25.

Mathematical homecoming you won't want to miss

Prof. Hugo Parlier, who recently left Luxembourg for Fribourg University, will be returning to Campus Belval for a special general audience talk in the Multiplicities Seminar on June 4, 2025.

Twelve Fantastic Stories

February 28, 2025

Mathematical mysteries now available in four languages

The book by Prof. Antonella Perucca and Dr. Lisa Vagnozzi, Twelve Fantastic Stories, is now freely available online in English, French, German, and Italian. In these engaging short stories, characters tackle mathematical riddles through collaboration and teamwork. Readers of all ages will discover twelve classical and accessible puzzles. To start with this mathematical adventure, visit the dedicated website.

SML Prizes awarded in December 2024

December, 2024

Top mathematical students honored at Luxembourg University

Congratulations to Béatrice Bach (Master in Secondary Education - Mathematics) and Camal Tahireddine for claiming the prestigious SML prizes for the best students in the Masters in Mathematics and Bachelor of Mathematics programs, respectively. These annual awards, sponsored by the SML, recognize outstanding academic achievement among University of Luxembourg students in mathematics.
